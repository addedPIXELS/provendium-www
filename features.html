Features

One system for the diary, the people and the work around them.

Provendium™ replaces the spreadsheets, documents and separate calendar tools a Province runs on today. Here is what it does, area by area.

The diary & your website

Plan once, publish everywhere.

The Provincial diary

Every Lodge and Chapter meeting, in one diary.

Craft and Royal Arch, regular meetings and special occasions, all planned from the same place. Each meeting records the business of the day, its venue and its time, and can carry more than one kind of event.

  • Draft, review and publish when it's ready
  • Cancel or reinstate a meeting, with notices sent
  • Recurring meetings generated from each unit's pattern

On your website

A calendar that lives on your Province's site.

Embed a widget that matches your website and shows the meetings you choose. It renders on your own page, refreshes on its own, and is locked to the domains you allow.

  • Month grid, list or compact view
  • Venue address, map and directions on each entry
  • Restricted to your domains with a signed token

Subscribe

Follow it in the calendar you already use.

Members can subscribe to a live feed and see meetings in Apple Calendar, Google Calendar or Outlook. When the diary changes, their calendar follows. There is nothing to download again.

  • The whole Provincial diary, or a single Lodge
  • The meetings a Provincial role is expected at
  • A private feed of your own meetings

The people

The registers, kept current.

Lodges & Chapters

Every unit and its full establishment.

Each Lodge and Chapter has its officers set out in the proper order of precedence: the whole establishment, shown filled and vacant, so a gap is obvious at a glance and easy to fill.

  • Officers ordered by Masonic precedence
  • Vacant offices shown inline, ready to assign
  • A searchable directory of every unit

Brethren

One record for each Brother, and his service over time.

A Brother appears once, wherever he serves. His offices and memberships build a career record that stays complete as he moves between Lodges and Orders. The history is the record, not a separate log to keep.

  • Offices and memberships, current and past
  • Search by unit, rank or office
  • Export a career summary, without contact details

Meetings & the festive board

From the invitation to the last place card.

Invitations & replies

Invited in one message, replies in one click.

Members are invited to the meetings that concern them and reply from the email itself, with no login and no password. They say whether they are attending and, separately, whether they are dining.

  • One-click reply from a secure link
  • Attendance and dining as two separate answers
  • Reminders chased automatically before the day

Seating & dining

The festive board, arranged and printed.

The dining numbers and dietary needs arrive with the replies. The DC team arranges the tables, keeps a plan everyone can work from, and prints the seating, the place cards and a dietary list for the kitchen.

  • Seating plan with a shared, lockable layout
  • Dietary requirements gathered and listed
  • Printable plan, place cards and kitchen list

The ceremonies team

A shared view for the Director of Ceremonies.

The DC team has its own diary and section: who is confirmed for each occasion, which of the team is covering it, and a briefing sheet to work from on the night.

  • Confirmed attendees per event
  • Team assignments across the diary
  • A briefing sheet for each occasion

Notices, documents & milestones

The paperwork around every meeting.

Summons & documents

The summons, kept with its meeting.

Upload the summons for a meeting and it stays with that meeting, ready for the members who should have it, not lost in an inbox or a shared drive.

  • One summons per meeting, stored securely
  • Available to the members it concerns

Notices & digests

The right people told, without the deluge.

Notices go to the members they concern, and a weekly digest gathers what's coming up. Sending at scale is guarded, so nobody is ever caught in a flood of duplicate messages.

  • Per-member notification preferences
  • A weekly digest of what's ahead
  • Safeguards on mass sends

Milestones

Centenaries, jubilees and other anniversaries are surfaced ahead of the date, so a Province can mark them properly and none slips by unnoticed.

  • Lodge and Chapter anniversaries
  • Members' Masonic jubilees
  • Flagged in advance, not on the day

Getting your data in

Start from the records you already keep.

Provendium can import a Province's existing membership records (Brethren, units, memberships, offices, ranks and the forward calendar), so there is no starting from a blank sheet. Imports are checked before they are applied, and can be re-run as the source changes.

The people who administer your Province recognise their own Lodges and Brethren from day one, which is the fastest way to know the system is right.
